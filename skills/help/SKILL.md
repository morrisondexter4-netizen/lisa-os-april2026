# Skill -- /help

**Trigger:** `/help`

Shows Lisa all available commands with plain-English descriptions and examples.

---

## Usage
```
/help
```

Or natural language: "Help", "What can you do?", "Show me the commands", "What are my options?"

---

## Display

> Here is everything I can help you with:
>
> ---
>
> **Getting Started**
> - **/setup** -- Walk through first-time setup: connect your email, calendar, and enter all your business details. Run this if anything needs to be reconfigured.
>
> ---
>
> **Daily Briefing**
> - **/briefing** -- Get a full rundown of everything that needs your attention: calendar for the week, pending invoices, students with sessions owed, travel clients, consulting days, and suggested next steps.
>   Example: "Catch me up" or "What do I need to know?"
>
> - **/recap** -- Weekly health check across all three businesses. Shows what happened, what is coming up, and what needs attention. Includes a money snapshot.
>   Example: "Give me a recap" or "How are things looking?"
>
> ---
>
> **Invoicing**
> - **/invoice [student name]** -- Create a tutoring invoice for a specific student. I will calculate the amount from their sessions and create a draft in your Gmail.
>   Example: "Invoice James Anthony" or "Bill Timothy Mullen"
>
> - **/invoice-harborfields [literacy, mtss, or sped]** -- Create a Harborfields district invoice for one of your three consulting verticals. Includes the PO number.
>   Example: "Invoice Harborfields for literacy" or "Run the MTSS invoice"
>
> ---
>
> **Session and Day Tracking**
> - **/add-sessions [student name] [number]** -- Log tutoring sessions for a student. Updates their record so invoicing stays accurate.
>   Example: "Add 3 sessions for James Anthony" or "Jack had a session today"
>
> - **/log-day [literacy, mtss, or sped]** -- Log a Harborfields consulting day. I will ask for the date and whether it was a full or half day.
>   Example: "Log a literacy day" or "I worked on MTSS today" or "Half day for special ed yesterday"
>
> ---
>
> **Email**
> - **/email** -- Draft any email. I will ask who it is to, what it is about, and what tone you want. Everything goes to your Gmail drafts -- I never send anything.
>   Example: "Draft an email to the Johnsons about rescheduling" or "Email Sarah about next Tuesday"
>
> ---
>
> **Your Businesses**
> - **/students** -- See all your Brightlings students with their rates, subjects, and sessions owed.
>   Example: "Show my students" or "Who owes sessions?"
>
> - **/clients** -- See your Quintessential Travel clients grouped by status (planning, booked, traveling, completed).
>   Example: "Show my travel clients" or "How are my clients doing?"
>
> ---
>
> **Schedule**
> - **/schedule** -- See your upcoming schedule for the next 7 days from your calendar and session records.
>   Example: "What is on my schedule?" or "Am I free Thursday?"
>
> ---
>
> **You do not need to memorize any of this.** Just talk to me like you would a real assistant. Here are some examples:
>
> - "Who owes me money?" -- I will check for unpaid invoices
> - "How many consulting days this month?" -- I will check your Harborfields records
> - "Add 3 sessions to Jack Marino" -- I will update his record
> - "I worked on literacy today" -- I will log a consulting day
> - "Catch me up" -- I will give you a full briefing
> - "New travel client named Maria" -- I will walk you through adding her
> - "Something is not working" -- I will troubleshoot and try to fix it
>
> Just ask!

---

## Rules
- Keep the format clean and scannable.
- Natural language examples are just as important as the slash commands.
- Never show file paths or technical details.
- If Lisa asks about a specific feature, answer that directly instead of showing the full help text.
